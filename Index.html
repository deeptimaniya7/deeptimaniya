Q1
Describe your key responsibilities in your Business Analyst role.

As a Business Analyst, my core responsibilities span the full requirement-to-delivery lifecycle. I conduct requirement gathering through stakeholder meetings, document the requirements, and share them with the client for confirmation. I then perform market research to validate whether competitors offer similar features, feed these insights into technical discussions, and prepare quotations for the sales team. On a daily basis, I maintain active communication with 10–15 clients and share timely updates via WhatsApp groups.

  • Conduct UAT for developed features
  • Assign tasks to the team
  • Mentor junior/new BAs, support and implementation teams
  • Manage follow-ups and handle unscheduled client escalations
  • Create project-specific documentation
  • Coordinate with external vendors and third-party service providers

Section C
Feedback & Expectations
Q9
What challenges do you face in your role that affect performance?
  • Resource Allocation: Limited resources in the BA department affect bandwidth and output quality.
  • Sales Team Commitments: False or unverified commitments to clients create expectation mismatches that the BA has to manage.
  • Product Knowledge Gaps: Implementation and support teams lack sufficient product knowledge, increasing dependency on BA.
  • No Fixed Workflow: Absence of a standardized workflow leads to inconsistency and inefficiency across projects.
  • Internal Process Follow-ups: Frequently reminding the organization about basic processes — assets, system access, payroll, increment — that should be managed proactively.
Q10
Do you have suggestions for improving BA processes, tools, or strategies?
  • Jira: Implement Jira for structured client and team communication to streamline project management.
  • AI Tools: Adopt AI-based tools to enhance requirement process flows, diagrams, research, and documentation quality.
  • Strategy 1: Support and Implementation teams should be the primary client contact; BA looped in only when a requirement arises.
  • Strategy 2: Shift from reactive client-driven development to a product vision-led approach — requirements considered but released only after thorough research, analysis, and development.
